Big Five Personality Test

This self-report inventory measures the Big Five personality traits using the IPIP Big-Five Factor Markers.

Background

The Big Five personality traits are widely accepted in academic psychology. They are derived through statistical factor analysis, identifying five major traits that describe human personality: extraversion, neuroticism, agreeableness, conscientiousness, and openness to experience.

This test is based on the Big-Five Factor Markers from the International Personality Item Pool, developed by Goldberg (1992).

Procedure

The test consists of 50 items rated on a five-point scale (1=Disagree, 3=Neutral, 5=Agree). Most people complete it in 3-8 minutes.

Participation

This test is for educational or entertainment purposes only. Responses are recorded anonymously and may be used for research.

Source: IPIP Big-Five Factor Markers

References: Goldberg, L.R. (1992). "The development of markers for the Big-Five factor structure." Psychological Assessment, 4(1), 26.
